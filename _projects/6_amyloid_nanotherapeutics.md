## Status: published

A published thread on **epigenetically active, NIR-responsive nanotherapeutics** aimed at the Aβ / α-synuclein axis of neurodegeneration.

**Key publications.**

- **Srivastava, A. K.**, Choudhury, S. R., Karmakar, S. *Near-infrared responsive dopamine/melatonin-derived nanocomposites abrogating in-situ amyloid beta nucleation, propagation, and ameliorating neuronal function.* **ACS Applied Materials & Interfaces** 12(5), 5658–5670 (2020).
- **Srivastava, A. K.**, Choudhury, S. R., Karmakar, S. *Melatonin/polydopamine nanostructures for collective neuroprotection in Parkinson's disease therapy.* **Biomaterials Science** (2019). [DOI: 10.1039/C9BM01602C](https://doi.org/10.1039/C9BM01602C).
- **Srivastava, A. K.**, Dev, A., Choudhury, S. R., Karmakar, S. *Curcumin-tethered lanthanum carbonate nanospheres and protein corona enable fluorescence-based detection of amyloid-β aggregates.* **Sensors and Actuators B: Chemical** 262, 1–9 (2018).

Together, these studies combined nanoparticle synthesis and characterization with quantitative Aβ aggregation kinetics, protein-corona analysis, mitochondrial and proteostasis readouts, and PD/AD neuronal-model validation — establishing a translational framework for epigenetically active nanotherapeutics.
