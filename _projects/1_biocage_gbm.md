## Status: current / in-development

The **Biocage** is a needle-sized porous implantable device fabricated by **two-photon polymerization (2PP) 3D printing** on a Nanoscribe system, using biodegradable **PEGDA hydrogel precursors**. The device is being developed for localized delivery of engineered neural stem cells and small-molecule payloads to the pediatric brain, with initial application to primary brain tumors that account for 20–25% of pediatric neoplasms.

**Approach.** Neural stem cells engineered to secrete tumor-selective effectors (for example, TRAIL) are seeded into the porous scaffold, which is designed to allow cell egress, drug diffusion, and eventual scaffold biodegradation. Biocompatibility and biodegradation kinetics are being characterized alongside *in vitro* efficacy in pediatric tumor cell models.

**Collaboration.** Microfabrication is carried out in collaboration with the Penn State Materials Research Institute.
