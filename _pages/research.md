My research is centered on three interconnected goals: ***modeling brain disease***, ***uncovering disease mechanisms***, and ***developing therapeutic strategies for neural repair***. I investigate a range of neurological disorders, including Parkinson’s disease, Alzheimer’s disease, epilepsy, Down syndrome, fetal alcohol spectrum disorders, and pediatric brain tumors. Across these conditions, I seek to understand how molecular and cellular abnormalities, including disrupted protein homeostasis, altered gene regulation, cellular stress, neuroinflammation, and impaired neurodevelopment, lead to dysfunction of neural circuits and brain networks. By integrating human stem cell derived models, animal models, and human brain tissue, my work aims to bridge molecular pathology with circuit level dysfunction. A major focus of my current research is understanding the mechanisms that drive network instability in epilepsy and neurodevelopmental disorders and translating these insights into novel therapeutic approaches, including cell based and biomaterial enabled strategies to restore healthy brain function.
