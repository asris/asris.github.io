Anup K. Srivastava

Research Postdoctoral Scientist Center for Neuroscience Research, Children's National Research Institute, Washington, DC.

I am Anup K. Srivastava, Ph.D., a Postdoctoral Scientist at the Center for Neuroscience Research, Children’s National Research Institute, Washington, DC. My research focuses on understanding the cellular and molecular mechanisms that drive neurodevelopmental and neurodegenerative disorders, with particular interest in how disruptions in protein homeostasis, epigenetic regulation, mitochondrial function, neuroinflammation, and inhibitory circuit development contribute to disease pathogenesis. I investigate how these alterations lead to abnormal neuronal connectivity, network hyperexcitability, and impaired brain function across different stages of development and disease progression.

My work spans a broad spectrum of neurological conditions, including Parkinson’s disease, Alzheimer’s disease, Dravet syndrome, Tuberous Sclerosis Complex, Focal Cortical Dysplasia, Down syndrome, fetal alcohol spectrum disorders, and pediatric brain tumors. By integrating insights from human brain tissue and advanced human stem cell–derived models, I aim to uncover convergent disease mechanisms and identify translational strategies that can promote neural repair, restore circuit function, and improve outcomes for patients with neurological disorders.

Outside research, I enjoy good conversations, mentoring young scientists, exploring new ideas, and finding inspiration in the small details of everyday life.
